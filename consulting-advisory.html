Information Privacy (POPIA & GDPR)

Protection of Personal Information Act (POPI Act) – POPIA aims to regulate the protection and access of personal information. This POPIA was enacted by the South African Parliament in 2013 and commenced application in 2020. Non-compliance with the Act could expose the Responsible Party to a penalty of a fine and/or imprisonment.

The General Data Protection Regulation (GRPR) – is a Regulation in the EU law on data protection and privacy in the EU and the European Economic Area. The GDPR is an important component of EU privacy law and of human rights law, in particular Article 8 of the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union.

Personal Information means any information relating to an identifiable, living natural person or juristic person (companies, CC’s) and includes, but is not limited to:

  • Contact details: email, telephone, address
  • Demographic information: age, sex, race, birth date, ethnicity
  • History relating to employment, financial, educational, criminal and medical details
  • Biometric information: blood type
  • Opinions of and about the person
  • Private correspondence

What We Do

Processing means anything that can be done with the Personal Information, including collection, usage, storage, dissemination, modification or destruction (whether such processing is automated or not).

Asset Verification Services

Our Asset Verification services provide businesses with the ability to accurately identify and track their assets, ensuring that they are in compliance with regulations and that they can be used for maximum efficiency. Our team of experts use advanced tools and techniques to verify and validate the existence, condition, and location of all assets, which can include physical assets such as equipment, vehicles, and real estate, as well as intangible assets such as patents and trademarks.

Our Asset Verification services include:

  • Physical Asset Verification: This process involves inspecting and documenting the existence, condition, and location of all physical assets.
  • Digital Asset Verification: This process involves identifying, verifying and validating the existence, ownership, and location of all digital assets, including software, websites, and databases.
  • Inventory Verification: This process involves identifying and verifying the existence, condition, and location of all inventory items.

Our Asset Verification services can help businesses to improve their asset management, reduce the risk of fraud, and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements. Additionally, we also provide ongoing maintenance and monitoring services to ensure that assets are always up-to date and that they can be used to their full potential. With our Asset Verification services, businesses can have peace of mind knowing that their assets are secure, compliant, and being used effectively.
